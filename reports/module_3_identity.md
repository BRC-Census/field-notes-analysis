# Module 3: The Mask vs. The Mirror (Identity)

**Research Question:** Do participants wear costumes to hide (Mask) or to reveal their true selves (Mirror)?

**Methodology:** Semantic classification of 114 responses regarding costume motivation from the 2024 dataset, segmented by **Age**. Sample size: Full Dataset.

## Results & Analysis
- **Play:** 53 (46.5%)
- **Authenticity:** 26 (22.8%)
- **Other:** 18 (15.8%)
- **Comfort:** 17 (14.9%)

### Age Analysis: Mask vs. Mirror
| Age Group | Authenticity (Mirror) | Disguise (Mask) | Play |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Under 30 | 12.5% | 0.0% | 43.8% |
| 30-39 | 28.9% | 0.0% | 46.7% |
| 40-49 | 27.3% | 0.0% | 54.5% |
| 50+ | 16.7% | 0.0% | 45.8% |

## Voices
- **Play:** *"I do. I like the transformation and the way others change."*

## Conclusion
> **Motivation Profile:** The most common motivation for wearing costumes is **Play** (46.5%). Play is the clear primary driver, with Authenticity (22.8%) as a secondary theme. Data suggests the costume functions more as a **Mirror** (Authenticity: 22.8%) than a **Mask** (Disguise: 0.0%).
